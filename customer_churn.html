Customer Churn Prediction Project

Published: December 3, 2025

Project Objective

The goal of this project is to predict customer churn for a telecom company and provide actionable insights to improve retention. By identifying customers at risk of leaving, the company can implement targeted strategies to reduce attrition, optimize marketing spend, and increase lifetime value.

Using a realistic dataset of 7,043 telecom customers, the project demonstrates challenges faced by analytics teams in retaining customers, including imbalanced classes, categorical and numerical data preprocessing, and deriving business insights.

Business Problem

Telecom companies face high churn rates, affecting revenue and growth. The challenge is to identify customers likely to churn and understand the main drivers of attrition:

  • Short tenure and month-to-month contracts
  • High monthly charges
  • Lack of additional services (TechSupport, OnlineSecurity)
  • Need for targeted retention strategies

Executive Summary

Customer churn is a significant challenge in the telecom sector, with 26.5% of customers at risk of leaving. Using the Telco dataset (7,043 customers), we built machine learning models to predict churn and uncover its key drivers.

Key outcomes:

  • XGBoost outperformed Logistic Regression, achieving 69% recall, effectively identifying most churners.
  • Main factors influencing churn: month-to-month contracts, short tenure, high monthly charges, and lack of TechSupport/OnlineSecurity.
  • Business recommendations include targeted retention campaigns, service improvements, and monitoring programs to maximize customer loyalty.

This project demonstrates the integration of predictive modeling with actionable business insights.

Dataset

The dataset contains customer information, account details, and service usage, including:

  • Customer demographics: Gender, SeniorCitizen, Partner, Dependents
  • Account info: Tenure, Contract, PaperlessBilling, PaymentMethod
  • Services: PhoneService, MultipleLines, InternetService, OnlineSecurity, OnlineBackup, DeviceProtection, TechSupport, StreamingTV, StreamingMovies
  • Charges: MonthlyCharges, TotalCharges
  • Target: Churn (Yes/No)

Introduction

Customer churn negatively affects revenue and growth. Understanding who is likely to leave and why is critical for telecom companies to retain customers and maintain profitability. This project uses historical customer data to build predictive models and guide business decisions.

Dataset Overview

  • 7,043 customers, 21 features
  • Features include demographic info, account details, and service usage
  • Target variable: Churn (Yes/No)

Challenges

  • Imbalanced dataset (73.5% non-churners, 26.5% churners)
  • Categorical and numerical data types requiring preprocessing
  • Need for actionable insights, not just predictive performance

Methodology

4.1 Data Preprocessing:

  • Converted TotalCharges to numeric after handling missing values.
  • Encoded categorical variables (binary encoding for Yes/No features, one-hot encoding for multi-class features).
  • Dropped customerID as non-predictive.

4.2 Feature Engineering:

  • Checked for outliers (none significant).
  • Split data into training (80%) and test (20%) sets.
  • Prepared features for model input.

4.3 Modeling Approach:

  • Logistic Regression: Baseline model for interpretability.
  • XGBoost: Tree-based model for improved predictive performance.

4.4 Evaluation Metrics:

  • Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1-score
  • Confusion Matrix to assess true/false positives and negatives

Insights:

  • Captures more at-risk customers → higher recall.
  • Slightly lower precision → some false positives, manageable for retention campaigns.
  • Aligns with business intuition and actionable strategies.

Conclusion

This project demonstrates how machine learning can be leveraged to predict customer churn and guide retention strategies. XGBoost effectively identifies at-risk customers, highlighting contract type, tenure, and service quality as primary churn drivers.

Recommendations:

  • Focus retention campaigns on high-risk customers (month-to-month, short tenure, high charges).
  • Promote TechSupport and OnlineSecurity services.
  • Improve service quality for high-value segments.
  • Monitor campaign outcomes and retrain models quarterly.

Impact:

By using predictive analytics to proactively address churn, companies can increase customer loyalty, reduce revenue loss, and optimize marketing investments.
